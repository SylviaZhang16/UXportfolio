Conclusion & Reflection

(1)In the compact team of the startup, my role as a UX designer often required direct input from the boss and frequent direct communication with customers and professional consultants, somehow assisting the product manager. This is a challenge and an opportunity for UX designers. The concerns of different stakeholders are often different, and as a designer, you need to work through brainstorming solutions and trial and error in the beginning stages, and in the iterative process, keep polishing a solution and validate it with stakeholders. And most importantly, the designer needs to explore the needs of the silent target users again behind these.

(2)Strict schedules and Jobs-to-be-done (JTBDs) can serve as important tools and communication platforms to advance cross-functional collaboration. For example, when communicating with development engineers, deliverables are needed to serve as objects and form milestones.
