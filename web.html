Problem Analysis

As a large country-owned museum, Shanghai Museum has explored the digitization of its collections and virtual exhibition to a certain extent. And its services are extensive and comprehensive, which shows its academic support and years of business accumulation.

However, there are several problem with the existing official websites and applications.
(1) The key features and exhibition resources are not well connected through different screens and devices. Users have to use all of them to receive the wanted service at the same time.
(2)The current interface of exhibits information, 3D models and correspondingly virtual exhibition is immature and hard to use. Also, they are not accessible on one certain mobile application.

Conclusion & Reflection

1. Solid user research is crucial. Quantitative analysis can reveal users' tendencies through statistics, while specific interviews can reveal users' pain points in more detail, thus building a vivid user portrait. In this regard, we hope to do more improvement and iteration in the future.
2. How far can the museum's virtual tour be developed in the future? Can it be combined with Bluetooth, NFC, and AR/VR technologies to achieve better results? With the growth of user experience design, it may be possible to get a more realistic and inclusive interactive experience through iteration.
3. What will be the further connection between the physical exhibition space and the virtual digital world? Will the physical exhibition space also change its composition as the user's viewing perspective and demand shifts? I think this is a question that museums and art practitioners will continue to explore together in the future.
