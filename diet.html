ByeJunk

Healthy diet management

Client

Freelance project
For Lemonbox

User experience designer

Deliverables

Duration

12 weeks

Process Overview

Goal Statement

User need

As a young white-collar worker, I was always under pressure to eat lots of junk food, so I wanted to control my appetite and shift to healthy recipes and lose weight.

Goal

Provide a product that controls junk food intake, providing pleasant feedback, and encouraging healthy eating habits from small to large.

Empathize - User Study

Interview

Key Problem

"I hate self-discipline!"

"I want instant happiness!"

□ No junk food brings misery. How do I get emotional rewards in diet?

□ So easy to give up and retaliatory eat. Can I really be more self-disciplined?

□ Various self-management skills are too complicated. How to get started easier?

Target User

Based on the frustrations of users during the three stages of diet management, we focus on the first two stages. Our product aims to provide hesitant outsiders and diet newbies with emotional opportunities and solutions as they move further in the process of controlling their eating cravings.
The first step is to help users "eat less junk food" and look for opportunities to progress to better healthy recipe management.

Persona

John and Chole are two representatives of our target user group. They tend to be young professionals who are busy and accumulate high levels of stress. In this context, junk food becomes a way to relieve stress. Rather than simply losing weight, their goal of diet management is closer to becoming a better version of themselves, whether self-driven or peer pressure-driven.

Design Challenge

Through the user study and the definition of target user, busy young white-collar workers, I understood their core concerns in controlling their junk food intake and identified 3 main design challenges.

Competitive Analysis

What kind of project shall we design? What features do we need?

Most of the competitive products focus on universal diet tracking and calculation. On one hand, these basic features are the core of the diet management app. On the other hand, they also bring tedious mechanical operations which can not attract the user to persevere and cultivate habits. In my opinion, I don’t regard them as a direct competitor of a junk food management product.
Therefore, we seek our own product features on this basis, narrowing the scope and focusing on junk food management.

Brainstorm & Ideation

Based on the 3 design challenges, I brainstorm helpful ideas and categorize them at the second level.

I summarized the groups of features and related ideas created in the ideation process and compared them on the two axes closely related to the design challenges.
As a result, 3 main design concepts/directions come out as a guide for prototype design. They arepositive & flexible motivation, pressure stimulation, spoon-feed guidance

The First Usability Test

Then I used these clickable prototypes and invited six participants to evaluate features under three different design directions. A system usability scale questionnaire was used to compare the functionality, usability, emotionality and effectiveness between the different features.

In the end, delayed wishlist, and Team/community task scored high in the test. Apparently, the participants found these two features both novel and interesting, and also more effective in encouraging people to persist in quitting junk eating and adopting healthy eating habits. Therefore, in the subsequent design of high-fidelity prototype, the unneeded features will be removed and these two features will be combined to build a lightweight self-management application.

Insights

Modified Key features

Based on user test reports, I further removed redundant features and focused on developing Delayed wishlists and Partner & Competition features. The Bonus system then serves as the basis for feature integration and positive incentives.
The overall focus of the product is on the psychology of users who are just starting to curb junk eating and develop healthy habits, offering a selection of easy and fun tools. As a result, users can have the confidence to further develop a more professional and comprehensive diet management.

User Flow

Bonus points provide incentives and limits for users to delay the desire of junk food, compete with partners and complete daily tasks. The mechanism behind this is simple: the more you are willing to stick to a healthy eating routine, the more you will be allowed some occasional "bad" cravings.
